# Halpha DESIGN.md

## Overview

Halpha is a quiet, evidence-centered market intelligence workbench. The UI should feel precise, bright, modern, analytical, and restrained. It must not look like a broker terminal, a consumer crypto exchange, a generic SaaS admin panel, or an AI-chat-first product.

The design model is:

```text
Black and white research desk
+ bright neutral surfaces
+ light operating rail
+ switchable signature accent
+ strict semantic status colors
+ artifact-first drilldown
+ smooth Apple-grade interaction feedback
```

The design goal is not visual decoration. The first screen should quickly answer:

```text
What is the current intelligence state?
What action level is appropriate?
What evidence supports it?
What risk blocks stronger action?
What data is missing, stale, partial, or degraded?
Which artifact proves the claim?
What changed after the last run?
```

Style attributes:

* bright and lightweight, not saturated or heavy;
* precise and operational, not promotional;
* evidence-first, not AI-magic-first;
* risk-aware, not signal-hunting;
* memorable through accent choice, not through decorative effects;
* local research workbench, not trading execution software.

Reports are output artifacts. They should not dominate product identity. Overview and workbench views should lead with current intelligence, risk limits, evidence, data coverage, strategy inspection, and monitor state.

## Colors

Use a black-and-white-first palette with one switchable structural accent. Most screens should read as black ink on white paper, with a light gray operating rail and sparse accent use.

### Core roles

* `primary` is the active theme accent. Use it for the primary action in a region, active navigation, selected segmented controls, range progress, focus rings, and small high-signal marks.
* `tertiary` is the companion accent. Use it for artifact links, secondary progress, inline emphasis, and chart overlays.
* `neutral`, `surface`, `surface-raised`, `surface-muted`, and `surface-inverse` define the bright surface stack.
* `text-primary`, `text-secondary`, and `text-muted` stay near black, slate, and gray. Do not tint body copy with the theme color.
* `on-accent` must be selected per theme. Use white text on blue, lilac, and graphite accents. Use dark ink on solar, amber, and ember accents.

### Theme families

Supported theme families:

```text
Mist       blue default; safest, most conventional
Lilac      soft purple analytical accent
Graphite   black and gray neutral control theme
Solar      yellow and black signature theme
Amber      orange and black operational theme
Ember      warm orange and black high-energy theme
```

Rules:

* Keep the global background black, white, or near-white.
* Use theme color only on key actions, selected states, focus, and progress accents.
* Do not flood panels, dashboards, or tables with theme color.
* Yellow and orange themes must stay bright and clean. Avoid muddy mustard, tan, brown, or espresso tones.
* Solar is the current default signature direction.
* Mist and Lilac remain safe alternate accents.
* Graphite is the neutral fallback for maximum seriousness.
* Theme switching must animate color and border changes without moving layout.

### Semantic colors

Semantic colors carry product meaning and are not decorative.

* Success: healthy state, valid coverage, completed run, available artifact.
* Warning: partial coverage, stale evidence, degraded input, limit state, pending confirmation.
* Danger: failed collection, blocked state, invalidation, high risk, avoid, exit, reduce.
* Info: metadata, bounded preview, neutral explanatory context.

Semantic colors may be lightly tuned per theme for harmony, but the meaning and hue family must remain stable. A warning cannot become green, and danger cannot become purple.

### Market chart colors

K-line candle colors are fixed and do not follow theme accents:

```text
Up candle     #0ECB81
Down candle   #F6465D
Buy marker    #0ECB81
Sell marker   #F6465D
```

This is intentional. Market chart readability and exchange-user expectation are more important than theme purity. Do not use green as a general success CTA or red as a generic destructive button just because K-line markers use those colors.

### Color prohibitions

* Do not use neon trading palettes across the UI.
* Do not use gradients, glow, glassmorphism, bokeh, or decorative color blobs.
* Do not make every badge colorful.
* Do not use color alone to explain decisions. Pair color with labels, evidence, and artifact links.
* Do not use muddy yellow-brown for progress, links, or secondary buttons.

## Typography

Use typography to create precision and scan speed.

Preferred UI stack:

```css
font-family: Inter, Geist, ui-sans-serif, system-ui, -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, "Segoe UI", sans-serif;
```

Preferred monospace stack:

```css
font-family: "JetBrains Mono", "IBM Plex Mono", ui-monospace, SFMono-Regular, Menlo, Monaco, Consolas, monospace;
```

Use monospace for:

* run IDs;
* timestamps;
* artifact filenames and paths;
* config keys;
* JSON keys;
* compact technical metadata.

Rules:

* Use tabular numbers for metrics, percentages, prices, durations, timestamps, and OHLC values.
* Set `letter-spacing` to `0` for all UI text.
* Use weight, line height, spacing, and tabular-number features for precision.
* Do not use negative tracking.
* Keep headings short and literal.
* Use bold sparingly. Dense panels should not feel shouted.
* Do not use marketing copy or playful labels for risk, alerts, or decisions.

Good UI copy:

```text
Risk elevated
Evidence incomplete
No high-quality action
Decision downgraded by missing derivatives data
```

Avoid:

```text
Amazing opportunity
AI thinks bullish
Guaranteed alpha
Strong buy signal
```

## Layout

Use a two-zone shell:

```text
Left: light operating rail
Right: bright research desk
```

### Left rail

The left rail contains:

* formal Halpha logo lockup;
* primary navigation;
* compact monitor or pipeline state when useful;
* latest run freshness when useful;
* a collapse control for dense review work.

Navigation order:

```text
Overview
Intelligence
Strategy
Reports
Monitor
Settings
```

Rules:

* The rail is stable and quiet. Do not make it a marketing sidebar.
* Use icon plus text navigation.
* Active navigation uses `primary`.
* Keep rail cards compact. They should not compete with main content.
* The rail must support a collapsed icon-only state on desktop and tablet.
* The collapsed state uses the same active navigation style, hides labels and rail copy, and preserves the monitor status dot.
* The collapse state may persist locally in browser storage. It is display state, not product data or runtime authority.
* The collapse toggle lives in the rail header, uses a current-color chevron, and must not look like a primary action.
* In collapsed or responsive compact rail state, do not show a separate expand chevron button. The Halpha logo mark becomes the rail header control.

### Main area

Use a disciplined 12-column desktop grid:

```text
24px page margin
16px standard grid gap
20px panel padding
main content plus right context rail when needed
```

Every page title belongs in the persistent top bar, not inside the page body.
The body should begin with the actual workflow content, filters, summary,
preview, chart, or artifact review surface.

The top bar is a page header:

```text
Left: current page title and optional short subtitle
Middle/right: contextual secondary tabs when the current page has them
No persistent runtime status text
No global refresh button
```

Secondary page tabs, such as Intelligence data types or Strategy operations,
sit in the top bar to the right of the title but remain left-aligned within
their available region. Do not push them to the far right.

If secondary tabs are wider than the available region:

* allow horizontal drag or trackpad scrolling;
* hide the scrollbar;
* show edge fade hints when more tabs are available;
* keep each tab label on one line;
* do not shrink labels until they truncate or wrap awkwardly.

The top bar must not show:

```text
Local mode
Timezone
Config path
Global refresh
```

Those values may exist in internal state or settings views, but they are not
persistent header content.

Overview priority:

```text
1. Current Intelligence
2. Decision Brief
3. Top Risks
4. Signal Conflicts
5. Data Coverage
6. Monitor State
7. Reports and Diagnostics
```

The top of the overview must not lead with report counts. Report operations belong below current intelligence and evidence.

For dense dashboard regions:

* Prefer table-like rows over nested cards.
* Keep metadata rows padded enough that separators do not crowd text.
* Prevent one-word wrapping in badges, filenames, and compact status cells.
* Use stable dimensions for boards, charts, controls, icon buttons, progress rows, and inspector tiles.
* Text must never overlap other text or overflow a control.
* Use `min-width: 0` on grid children and flex children that may contain dynamic text.
* Use `overflow-wrap: anywhere` for long run IDs, artifact names, URLs, source refs, and warning text.
* Toolbar and control groups must wrap before they overflow.
* Buttons in dense toolbars may wrap text to two lines; they must not push outside their parent.

### Composition containers

Composition containers arrange child components. They are not cards.

Examples:

```text
page content grids
tab panel bodies
strategy operation panels
intelligence overview panels
preview layouts
left-middle-right inspection layouts
```

Rules:

* Composition containers use transparent background, no border, no shadow, and zero radius.
* Do not put a border around a container that already contains bordered cards, metric strips, preview panes, or controls.
* Child components own their own surface, padding, border, and status state.
* Use `gap` to separate children instead of a parent background color.
* If a parent needs visual grouping, use a muted band or titled section, not another card shell.
* Do not create cards inside cards. Use rows, bands, or muted blocks inside panels.

### Metric grids

Metric groups must not look like unpadded spreadsheet cells.

Rules:

* Use a padded grid surface around repeated metric cards.
* The grid surface uses `surface-muted`, 1px border, 14px radius, and 8px padding.
* Repeated metric cards use white surface, 1px border, 14px radius, and at least 14px padding.
* Use `gap` between cards. Do not rely on adjacent borders or zero-gap cell seams.
* Use `repeat(auto-fit, minmax(...))` so narrow panels become 2x2 or 1-column layouts before text is squeezed.
* Metric cards must have stable heights for loading and loaded states.

### Responsive behavior

* At tablet width, reduce columns before reducing readability.
* At mobile width, stack panels in source order.
* Keep primary actions reachable without covering persistent top-right controls.
* Preserve chart minimum height where interaction matters.

## Elevation & Depth

Use borders and tonal layers before shadows.

Depth model:

```text
Base: bright neutral background
Panels: paper white plus 1px quiet border
Muted blocks: light gray-blue or light gray surface
Overlays: subtle shadow plus quiet backdrop
Focus: theme accent ring
```

Rules:

* Dashboard panels should not use heavy shadows.
* Use shadow for drawers, popovers, command palettes, and centered toast only.
* Do not use translucent blur as a primary surface style.
* Use elevation only to clarify interaction state.
* Raised demo tokens must not be editable or text-selectable unless they are actual inputs.

Recommended panel style:

```css
background: #FFFFFF;
border: 1px solid var(--border);
border-radius: 14px;
box-shadow: none;
```

Recommended overlay style:

```css
background: #FFFFFF;
border: 1px solid var(--border);
border-radius: 18px;
box-shadow: 0 18px 48px rgba(42, 60, 95, 0.16);
```

## Shapes

Use rational, restrained geometry.

Radius scale:

```text
4px    small tags, table marks, technical chips
6px    compact inputs and small controls
10px   buttons, nav items, segmented controls
14px   panels and cards
18px   drawers, command palette, date-time composer
999px  pills, switches, range thumbs
```

Rules:

* Avoid overly round SaaS shapes.
* Avoid sharp brutalist corners.
* Keep cards, inputs, buttons, chips, and panels geometrically consistent.
* Cards should use 14px or less unless an overlay needs extra softness.
* Do not place cards inside cards. Use rows, bands, or muted blocks inside panels.

### Logo

Use the formal Halpha monogram as the primary mark.

Rules:

* Light surface: black mark.
* Dark surface: warm-white mark.
* Do not place the logo inside a colored rounded square except for favicon or app-icon fallback.
* Do not add gradient, glow, shadow, bevel, or outline.
* Preserve clearspace.
* Brand lockup should be minimal: mark plus `Halpha` plus optional descriptor.
* Descriptor may be `Market Intelligence` or `Research Workbench`.

## Components

### Interaction model

Interaction should feel operating-system-grade: immediate, smooth, quiet, and physically coherent. It should resemble Apple-style interaction quality without becoming playful or decorative.

Core rule:

```text
Every operation acknowledges immediately, shows where work is happening, and resolves visibly.
```

Use the same feedback ladder for every control:

```text
1. Available   default affordance
2. Hover       subtle surface or border change
3. Press       tiny scale or compression
4. Commit      selected, busy, opened, or scrolled-to state
5. Resolve     toast, inline status, drawer, completed progress, or error
```

Required states:

```text
default
hover
pressed
focus-visible
selected / aria-pressed
busy / aria-busy
disabled
resolved
```

Motion rules:

* Click feedback must appear in the same interaction frame.
* Press scale should be around `0.985`.
* Use `90ms` for minor dismissal, `150ms` for hover and compact states, `220ms` for standard state transitions, and `420ms` for drawers, command palette, target spotlight, and larger transitions.
* Preferred easing: `cubic-bezier(0.2, 0.8, 0.2, 1)` and `cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)`.
* Animate opacity, transform, border color, background color, and progress width.
* Avoid animating layout dimensions unless a component is explicitly expanding or collapsing.
* Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.

### App shell

The app shell should establish identity immediately:

```text
Light operating rail
Formal logo
Bright main surface
Persistent page-title top bar
Evidence-first content
```

The top bar should show:

```text
Current page title
Optional short subtitle
Contextual secondary tabs
```

The top bar should not show:

```text
Local mode
Timezone
Active config path
Global refresh button
Long status copy
```

Keep the top bar quiet. It must not cover centered feedback, and it must not
become a runtime telemetry strip. Runtime state belongs in Overview, Monitor,
Settings, page-local status controls, issue drawers, or workflow progress
surfaces.

### Buttons and actions

Use one primary action per region.

Primary actions:

```text
Run reassessment
Generate report
Run evaluation
Start monitor
Run sample job
```

Secondary actions:

```text
Open artifact
Review data coverage
Open latest report
Open source map
Open summary
```

Rules:

* Primary buttons use `primary`.
* Secondary buttons use paper surface and border.
* Artifact actions can use `tertiary`, but should stay quieter than primary actions.
* Destructive actions use danger styling and confirmation.
* Do not use green CTA buttons by default.
* Busy buttons show a local moving progress strip. The strip should travel left to right or cleanly sweep across the full button, not oscillate in a cramped corner.
* Long-running actions must also set `aria-busy`.
* Refresh actions are page-local or region-local. Do not add a persistent global refresh button to the top bar.
* Collection, export, backtest, and report actions live with the workflow they operate on, not in global navigation.

### Navigation and selection

Use tabs, segmented controls, and nav items for mutually exclusive states.

Rules:

* Selected state is persistent and explicit.
* Segmented controls update visible content immediately.
* Use `aria-current` for active navigation and `aria-pressed` for toggle-like controls.
* Hover and press states must not resize the control.
* Vertical section selectors should use the same tab language as horizontal tabs: neutral surface, compact gap, bordered group, selected item on `primary`.
* Do not use unrelated accent colors for nested navigation. A green active state is invalid in the solar theme unless it carries a semantic success meaning.
* Chevron, caret, and disclosure icons must inherit current text color and use the same stroke style as the rest of the shell icons.
* Active navigation, active tabs, active segmented controls, and selected settings sections must look related.
* Page-level secondary tabs belong in the top bar when they switch major sections inside a page, such as Intelligence data types or Strategy operation modes.
* Top-bar tabs are left-aligned in the tab region. Do not right-align the tab group.
* Top-bar tabs use a bordered neutral group with `primary` selected state.
* Top-bar tabs may scroll horizontally when space is limited, but visible scrollbars are not allowed.
* Use an edge fade or equivalent subtle mask to communicate hidden tabs. Do not use arrows unless the tab set cannot be dragged or trackpad-scrolled.
* Dragging a tab strip must not accidentally activate a tab. Activation occurs on click or keyboard selection, not on drag.
* Page-level tab labels should be short enough for the header. The full semantic name remains in the panel title or content when the tab label is abbreviated.
* Do not place refresh buttons, source selectors, or workflow inputs in the page-level tab group.
* Primary side navigation must support expanded and collapsed states. Expanded uses icon plus text; collapsed uses icons only and preserves active state.
* Collapsing the side rail is a local view preference. It must not change routes, reset filters, reload data, or create product state.
* In a collapsed or responsive compact rail, the logo mark is the only rail header target. Avoid placing an expand arrow beside or on top of the mark.
* Settings and similar vertical subsection selectors use the same tab visual language instead of colored list menus.

### Sliders, switches, and inputs

Ranges must look tactile and precise.

Rules:

* Range thumbs are raised circles with a light border and subtle shadow.
* Continuous sliders do not show ticks.
* Discrete sliders show tick marks only when the number of stops is small enough to read, approximately 12 or fewer.
* Unreached ticks should be visible but quiet; they should be slightly stronger than the bare track.
* At min and max, the fill must visually reach the endpoint without leaving a sliver of track.
* Outputs should update live and stay close to the control.
* Inputs, selects, and textareas use white surface, quiet border, 10px radius, and clear focus ring.
* Checkbox chips and cleanup options use a custom square check mark, not browser-default blue or green styling.
* Checked state uses `primary` with dark `on-accent` mark for the solar theme.
* Multi-select chip groups must wrap as a grid and preserve readable labels.

### Date, time, and range controls

Date-time controls should feel like first-class product components, not browser defaults pasted into the dashboard.

Required pieces:

```text
Start date and time
End date and time
Timeframe
Timezone
Quick presets
Readable range summary
```

Rules:

* Use structured fields for date, time, timeframe, and timezone.
* Use a unified composer surface with 18px radius.
* Calendar day cells must have stable square dimensions.
* Selected start/end days use `primary`.
* In-range days use `info-bg` and `info-text`.
* Time wheels use the same selected and hover language as the calendar.
* Presets use segmented control styling.
* Date and range popovers use one outer surface only. Preset rows, time rows, and action rows must not add their own gray panel backgrounds, nested borders, or shadows.
* Separate popover internals with `gap` and whitespace, not adjacent borders or zero-gap seams.
* Action rows contain actions only. Do not place helper text beside action buttons where it can be clipped; use labels, tooltips, or clear control copy instead.
* The summary should state the actual window and candle interval.

### Toasts and feedback surfaces

Toast feedback is centered near the top of the main viewport, not in the top-right corner. The top-right area is reserved for persistent global actions.

Rules:

* Toasts use a paper surface, quiet border, subtle shadow, and short pop motion.
* The left accent bar uses message tone:
  * info -> `info`
  * success -> `success`
  * warning -> `warning`
  * danger -> `danger`
* Toasts confirm completion, failure, or important state change. They do not replace local state.
* Toast copy must be concrete:

```text
Reassessment complete. Action remains WATCH due to partial coverage.
Coverage impact highlighted.
Collection failed and needs review.
```

Avoid:

```text
Done
Success
Great
```

### Drawers, command palette, and overlays

Drawers and command palettes should:

* preserve dashboard context with a quiet backdrop;
* open with short emphasized motion;
* focus the first useful control;
* include stable close controls;
* support `Escape`;
* return the user to the previous context after close.

Artifact drawers must show summaries first and bounded source detail second. Do not dump full raw artifacts by default.

Properties and metadata panels should be right-side drawers when they would
steal horizontal room from the main reading surface. This is the default for
Intelligence record properties.

Rules:

* Keep the main content area wide enough for readable text, charts, or previews.
* Place the properties entry in the local title or header action area.
* The drawer starts with a human summary, then structured attributes.
* Long values wrap inside the drawer body, not in small badges or table cells.
* Do not keep a permanent right properties column when the same information can live in a drawer.
* Collection configuration that is not part of ordinary browsing opens in a dialog. Preview filters stay on the page; collection parameters and coverage timeline stay in the collection dialog.

### Badges and status language

Badges must be semantic.

* Neutral: watch, default, metadata, no action.
* Success: healthy, completed, valid.
* Warning: degraded, stale, partial, missing, pending.
* Danger: failed, blocked, invalidated, avoid, exit, reduce.
* Info: contextual metadata, bounded preview.

Rules:

* Do not use badges as decoration.
* Keep badge text short and non-overlapping.
* Use labels first. Color supports meaning but does not replace text.
* Warnings and issue counts should appear as compact header controls when details are secondary.
* Large records/range/coverage summary blocks should not duplicate information already visible in timelines, filters, or panel titles.
* Clicking an issue count should open a bounded popover, drawer, or detail panel with the concrete warnings and errors.
* A warning badge must never overflow its card; long messages wrap inside the details surface, not inside the badge.

### Data coverage cards

Data coverage cards must explain decision impact.

Required fields:

```text
Source or artifact
Coverage state
Freshness
Missing or stale fields
Decision impact
Suggested action
Artifact link
```

Coverage states:

```text
complete
partial
stale
missing
degraded
not configured
```

When multiple coverage entries are shown inside one region, prefer compact rows or table-like layout over nested cards.

### Timeline with annotations

Timeline components show coverage, states, or process history across a coordinate axis.

Rules:

* Put the coordinate labels below the track and close to it so labels align visually with segments and markers.
* Use a single visible track with colored segments.
* Marker labels sit above or beside the marker without covering the track.
* Use semantic colors for collected, partial, failed, and bounded preview states.
* Legends stay below the track and use both color and text.
* Do not place date labels far above the timeline or separated from the plotted coordinate system.

### Finite progress and operation logs

Finite progress is for bounded tasks with known completion.

Rules:

* Show percent, label, and source or step context.
* Use `primary` for main progress, `success` for validated completion, `warning` for degraded or partial work.
* Keep progress bars slim, bright, and horizontally aligned.
* Operation logs should show step, state, timestamp, and short diagnostic text.
* Log filters should be compact and preserve current state.
* Do not hide partial success.

### Tables and rows

Rules:

* Compact rows with enough padding around separators.
* Left-align labels and filenames.
* Right-align numbers.
* Use tabular numbers.
* Use subtle row separators, not heavy grids.
* Long artifact filenames should truncate or wrap cleanly without overlapping status badges.
* Inline metadata should use gaps, not crowded vertical separators.

### Charts

Rules:

* Always show time window and source.
* Label watch triggers, invalidation levels, and regime changes.
* Use muted grid lines.
* Avoid 3D, gradients, and decorative animation.
* Do not use unlabeled sparklines for important conclusions.
* Use mature chart libraries for complex market interaction rather than hand-rolled canvas or SVG.

### K-line chart

The K-line component is a primary Strategy Lab surface and should follow a mature exchange-grade interaction model while retaining Halpha's research visual style.

Required behavior:

```text
Pan left and right
Mouse wheel zoom
Crosshair with vertical and horizontal axis labels
Hover OHLC detail
Volume panel
Moving averages
MACD or other indicator panel when useful
Buy, sell, event, funding, warning, and gate markers where available
Range controls such as 1D / 7D / 30D
Inspector panel for selected candle details
```

Rules:

* Use fixed candle colors: up `#0ECB81`, down `#F6465D`.
* Use fixed trade marker colors: buy `#0ECB81`, sell `#F6465D`.
* Trade markers use a compact Binance-style pentagon, not a plain triangle or oversized pin.
* Marker width must not exceed the visible candle body width.
* Buy markers sit below the candle and point toward it.
* Sell markers sit above the candle and point toward it.
* If one candle has multiple buy markers or multiple sell markers, show one visual marker per side and show the count in hover detail.
* Hover detail and the inspector must show buy and sell marker state for the selected candle.
* Keep chart background on `surface-muted`.
* Use right-side y-axis labels for price and indicator panes.
* Use muted grid lines and high-contrast crosshair labels.
* Show markers as research annotations, not trading instructions.
* Do not theme candle or trade marker red/green with the active accent.

### Report review surfaces

The Reports view is an artifact review workflow, not a file explorer. It should
let the user read the final report and inspect the bounded evidence files that
support it without leaving the report context.

Required layout:

```text
Left rail: report library
Main surface: selected report or selected source file reader
Right rail: report outline first, report source files second
```

Rules:

* The generated report file is pinned at the top of the source list and is the
  way back to the report body.
* The outline remains a navigation aid for the currently rendered main reader.
* Source files are grouped by durable run-local categories such as Report,
  Analysis, Codex context, Raw inputs, Run metadata, and Other.
* Source rows show a readable title, bounded run-relative path, and compact size
  or kind metadata.
* Selected source rows use the same `primary` selected-state language as tabs
  and navigation. Do not use green active states unless the row itself is a
  semantic success state.
* Report details stay in a right-side drawer. Do not reserve a permanent
  metadata column that narrows the report reader.
* Source browsing must not expose absolute machine paths, private config
  locations, or raw local private values.

### Artifact readers

Artifact readers convert bounded source previews into human-readable evidence
surfaces. They must not dump raw files into the main reader by default.

Rules:

* Markdown is rendered as reading content.
* JSON and JSONL are rendered as structured fields, tables, and summaries when
  the bounded preview can be parsed.
* CSV is rendered as a table with bounded rows.
* Text, YAML, and logs are rendered as paragraph or line blocks, not as a raw
  full-file code dump.
* Oversized or truncated structured files show a bounded-preview explanation
  instead of displaying invalid partial JSON as raw text.
* Unsupported binary or database-like files show an explicit unsupported state.
* The reader header shows category, title, source ref, kind, and size where
  available.
* Long source refs, URLs, JSON keys, and paths wrap inside the reader without
  overlapping buttons, badges, or adjacent columns.

### AI explanation panel

AI is an explanation surface, not the source of decision state.

Rules:

### Loading, empty, and error states

Dynamic pages must render a stable structure before network data arrives. Empty dynamic containers are not acceptable for first paint.

Loading states should name the pipeline step when a text label is needed:

```text
Updating market data
Evaluating risk
Generating decision material
Writing report
Checking data coverage
```

Skeleton loading is the default for dashboard panels that are filled by API data.

Rules:

* Use skeleton cards, rows, lines, timeline tracks, and chart placeholders to reserve the approximate final layout.
* The skeleton layout should match the loaded component's grid, padding, and height closely enough that the page does not jump when data resolves.
* Use a subtle shimmer on neutral surfaces. Do not use spinners as the primary loading pattern for large dashboard regions.
* Set `aria-busy="true"` on the active view or busy component while data is loading.
* Disable pointer events on placeholder content.
* Loading placeholders must not cover or disable unrelated global navigation.
* On repeated navigation, keep recently loaded content visible instead of replacing it with skeletons unless the user forced a refresh.
* For dashboard view switching, short-lived cached read models are preferred over immediate refetch when the data is still fresh.
* Merge concurrent refreshes for the same view instead of issuing duplicate API requests.
* A forced refresh should show progress but preserve major layout dimensions.
* Skeletons must respect `prefers-reduced-motion` by disabling or shortening animation.

Empty states should be specific and actionable:

```text
No report has been generated yet.
No derivatives source configured.
No alert decisions available.
```

Every error state must show:

```text
What failed
Affected artifact or module
Whether the run is partial or failed
Suggested next action
Link to manifest or logs
```

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do

* Lead with current intelligence, not report counts.
* Use bright neutral surfaces and black structural mass.
* Use the active theme accent sparingly.
* Keep semantic colors strict and meaningful.
* Make every operation acknowledge, commit, and resolve visibly.
* Put toast feedback in the centered top area.
* Show local busy feedback near the initiating control.
* Make major claims drillable to artifacts.
* Show uncertainty, degraded state, missing data, and conflict.
* Distinguish market risk from system failure.
* Show what changed since the previous run.
* Use tabular numbers and aligned metrics.
* Prefer borders over heavy shadows.
* Use mature chart components for K-line interaction.
* Treat AI as explanation, not primary judgment.
* Put page titles in the persistent top bar.
* Put page-level secondary tabs next to the title, left-aligned within the top bar.
* Hide horizontal tab scrollbars and use edge fades to show there is more content.
* Preserve a collapsible side rail for dense analysis workflows.
* Use the logo mark as the collapsed rail's expand control.
* Use drawers for secondary properties so the main reading area stays wide.
* Put collection setup in a dialog when it is not the primary browsing task.

### Don't

* Do not make Halpha look like a generic SaaS dashboard.
* Do not make Halpha look like a broker, exchange, or execution terminal.
* Do not imply automatic trading, guaranteed alpha, or account execution.
* Do not use neon green/red trading aesthetics outside the K-line candle convention.
* Do not use green as a buy button or red as a sell button in product controls.
* Do not lead overview with `Total reports`.
* Do not hide evidence behind AI text.
* Do not let AI chat dominate the UI.
* Do not show strong action without risk, confidence, invalidation, and evidence.
* Do not use gradients, glow, glassmorphism, decorative blobs, or 3D charts.
* Do not overuse colorful status pills.
* Do not show missing data as a generic warning without decision impact.
* Do not create dashboard widgets that do not map to stable artifacts.
* Do not display sensitive config values unnecessarily.
* Do not brand runtime or deployment mode as a product feature.
* Do not add visual complexity before the intelligence contract is clear.
* Do not show `Local mode`, timezone, or config path in the persistent top bar.
* Do not add a persistent global refresh button to the top bar.
* Do not right-align page-level tab groups.
* Do not show visible scrollbars for top-bar tab overflow.
* Do not place preview filters and collection parameters in the same control band.
* Do not reserve permanent columns for metadata that can live in a drawer.
* Do not show a separate expand chevron button in the collapsed or responsive compact rail header.
